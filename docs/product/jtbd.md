# JTBD: Just Build with Blocks Signature Product

Date: 2026-03-06
Repository: Just-Build-Blocks

## Core Job Statement

When I want to show what a brand or idea would look like as a premium block set, I want to start from a logo image or a name and end with a buildable, film-ready collectible, so I can share something that feels complete instead of hypothetical.

## Primary Jobs

1. Turn a known logo or named brand into a believable premium block-set object.
2. Produce a full artifact set, including box, studio, instructions, exports, and film, from one coherent system.
3. Make the result feel toy-native and instantly legible without relying on official brand names or copied visual systems.

## Job Steps

| Step | Current friction | Desired progress |
|---|---|---|
| Discover | Most concept work starts as disconnected stills or vague prompts | The experience starts from a familiar logo upload or brand-name entry |
| Decide | Users do not know how much detail or art direction they need | The app chooses a strong collectible direction with sane defaults |
| Execute | Building box, model, instructions, and film usually means separate tools | One pipeline generates the set, the wrapper, the studio state, and the exports |
| Verify | It is easy for a concept to look good but feel fake or incomplete | The object looks buildable, the exports are consistent, and the film lands cleanly |
| Maintain | Later storytelling and export work can drift away from the original concept | The same canonical set data continues to drive instructions, exports, and Remotion scenes |

## Outcome Expectations

- Speed: recognizable input to strong first frame in under a minute on the happy path.
- Confidence: the result feels like a premium set system, not just a polished poster.
- Completeness: the user can move from set-box reveal to studio, instructions, exports, and film without the product changing its story.

## Forces of Adoption

### Push (pain)

- Brand and creative teams often need a product-looking artifact, not just another concept deck.

### Pull (benefits)

- The experience makes a brand feel physically buildable, memorable, and ready for storytelling.

### Anxiety (risk)

- The output might look counterfeit, generic, or too fake to believe as a real set.

### Habits (status quo)

- Teams default to static mockups, mood boards, and manual motion treatments stitched together from separate tools.
