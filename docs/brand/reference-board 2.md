# Just Build with Blocks Reference Board

Date: 2026-03-06

## Core direction

The product should feel like a premium OpenAI-built block-set generator with a toy-native wrapper, not like a toy-brand clone and not like a generic file uploader.

Use this board to keep the visual system disciplined:

- the shell stays calm, light, and precise
- the wrapper carries delight and guidance
- the hero object stays believable and collectible
- the upload flow teaches the user what makes a good result

## Product surfaces

### Shell

- Mood: OpenAI-clean, precise, quiet confidence
- Palette: white, graphite, restrained blue, pale gray working surfaces
- Role: navigation, input flow, instructions, exports
- Watch-out: if the shell starts feeling like a toy website, it is too loud

### Set box

- Mood: immediate excitement
- Palette: blue hero field, red badge, yellow accent, white metadata
- Role: first magical payoff after upload or prompt
- Watch-out: avoid source-identical packaging structure or retail mimicry

### Builder studio

- Mood: guided creative play
- Palette: white shell plus trays, boards, and a disciplined set object
- Role: the "I could actually build this" moment
- Watch-out: should feel like a guided 3D toy, not CAD software

### Build sheet

- Mood: satisfying clarity
- Palette: pale blue or white page field, graphite type, restrained accent color
- Role: make the output feel real, not merely cinematic
- Watch-out: avoid faux-manual nostalgia that drifts into imitation

### Keepsake

- Mood: proud, shareable, quietly aspirational
- Palette: keep the set-box energy, but let the shell breathe
- Role: stills, instruction artifact, manifests, exports, and film
- Watch-out: commerce stays teaser-only until there is a real fulfillment story

## Elements worth incorporating

### 1. Teach before upload

The strongest idea in the screenshots is not the styling. It is the product behavior. The flow teaches the user what makes a good input before expecting a strong output.

Patterns to borrow:

- a dedicated "how to get a good result" path near the uploader
- positive and negative examples shown side by side
- simple reasons for success or failure
- one clear upload action after the user understands the rules

For us, this should become logo-quality guidance:

- strong silhouette
- clean contrast
- limited detail at small scale
- enough rights to use the mark or image

### 2. Oversized upload target

The large empty drop zone creates confidence. It makes the next action obvious and gives the product room to feel premium.

Patterns to borrow:

- one large square or near-square upload field
- one primary button inside the field
- low-noise file constraints below the field
- help content in a separate column rather than inside the upload box

For us, the upload area should feel more gallery-clean than toy-retail loud. The wrapper can show up in accents, not by covering the whole screen in color.

### 3. Guided image-quality examples

The preview examples are useful because they show the transformation result, not just the source image.

Patterns to borrow:

- preview the processed build next to example source inputs
- show pass and fail states with simple visual markers
- explain why a source works in plain language
- use navigation arrows to move through a short teaching sequence

For us, this is especially important because our goal is more ambitious than a flat mosaic. We should teach what helps a logo become a believable buildable set.

### 4. Friendly progress state

The loading screen works because it feels deliberate and human. It turns waiting into part of the product.

Patterns to borrow:

- one branded loading state
- one line of progress copy
- one friendly visual anchor
- no noisy spinners or dashboard chrome

For us, the visual anchor should be original. A block-built Codex guide, collectible object, or simple studio robot would fit better than any figure silhouette that feels too close to a known toy brand.

### 5. Calm canvas with playful edge details

The screenshots use a quiet gray field, lots of whitespace, and a few bright accent objects at the edges. That balance is useful.

Patterns to borrow:

- large pale working area
- high-contrast type with generous spacing
- one hot accent color for actions
- cropped corner objects to add atmosphere without cluttering the main task

For us, corner details could become abstract studded forms, trays, or block geometry rendered in our own style system.

### 6. Compliance gate as a product surface

The terms step is visually plain, but the product idea is important: legal and rights guidance is part of the flow, not hidden in a footer.

Patterns to borrow:

- rights confirmation before upload or export
- plain-language explanation of what is allowed
- one checkbox and one continue action
- calm layout instead of a legal-looking wall of modal chrome

For us, this should be adapted to brand and logo usage:

- the user confirms they own the mark or have the right to use it
- the user confirms they are not asking for counterfeit retail packaging
- the product explains that outputs are original block-set interpretations, not official branded goods

## Elements to avoid copying

- official wordmarks, names, taglines, or product titles
- figure designs or silhouettes that read like a known minifigure system
- exact yellow-gray page compositions
- exact copy tone, headline wording, or helper text
- exact mosaic preview framing or carousel behavior
- direct reuse of acceptance or rejection language

## What this means for our product

### Upload flow

We should incorporate:

- a large upload target
- a nearby guide for what makes a strong logo or image
- a legal-safe rights confirmation step
- one strong call to action

### Quality coaching

We should incorporate:

- positive and negative source examples
- processed preview examples, not just input thumbnails
- short reasons such as "clean silhouette," "too low contrast," and "too detailed at small scale"

### Visual language

We should incorporate:

- generous whitespace
- large, friendly typography
- bright accent actions against a calm shell
- subtle decorative block geometry around the edges

### Brand-safe adaptation

We should keep:

- the clarity
- the teaching behavior
- the welcoming upload posture
- the transformation-preview pattern

We should change:

- all branding
- all characters or mascot logic
- the exact accent hierarchy
- the wording
- the output framing so it serves buildable set generation, studio phases, instructions, and film
