# Just Build with Blocks Brand Guidelines

Date: 2026-03-05

## Positioning

`Just Build with Blocks` should feel like an OpenAI product that unexpectedly grew into a custom block-set creator.

- OpenAI owns the shell.
- Blocks own the delight.
- The shell is clean, white, exact, and calm.
- The stage is bright, primary, glossy, and memorable.

## Product promise

- Upload an image or describe an idea.
- Reveal a custom `BLOCKS` set box.
- Step into a guided builder studio.
- Leave with parts, instructions, stills, and a film-ready handoff.

## Shell vs stage

### OpenAI shell

- OpenAI Sans everywhere.
- White, graphite, and restrained blue accents.
- Clear hierarchy.
- Generous spacing.
- No editorial beige, gallery language, or mood-board softness.

### Block-set stage

- Big blue packaging field.
- Red `BLOCKS` badge.
- Bright yellow accents.
- Chunky trays, studs, and glossy plastic surfaces.
- Strong shadows and playful scale.

## Legal-safe guardrails

The emotional target is “blink twice” familiarity, not counterfeit mimicry.

Do not ship:

- official LEGO logos or wordmarks
- source-identical typefaces
- copied packaging layouts
- set-number systems that mirror retail products
- instruction-book pastiche that feels one-to-one
- minifigure references or silhouettes

Do ship:

- an original `BLOCKS` badge
- original metadata labels such as `Builder age`, `Build ID`, `Pieces`, and `Mode`
- original cover composition
- original instruction language and studio UI

## Tokens

### Color roles

- `Studio White` `#FFFFFF`
- `Graphite Ink` `#101828`
- `Signal Red` `#C4281C`
- `Orbit Blue` `#0055BF`
- `Bright Yellow` `#FFD500`
- `Builder Green` `#00C853`

### Typography

- Display: `OpenAI Sans`
- UI: `OpenAI Sans`
- Labels and counts: `OpenAI Sans`
- No secondary display face

### Shape language

- Shell: disciplined rounded rectangles
- Stage: chunkier rounded forms with stud rhythm
- Controls: slightly blocky, never retro-game literal
- Surfaces: glossy, tactile, layered, and obviously physical

## Copy voice

- warm
- direct
- short
- quietly ambitious
- playful without sounding childish

Preferred lines:

- Upload it. Or describe it. Build the set.
- Drop a mark. Build a world.
- The software is the board. The blocks are the possibilities.

## Motion rules

- snap
- slide
- settle
- click
- reveal

Avoid:

- liquid motion
- goo
- hologram tropes
- overblown trailer behavior

## Product bar

Every sprint point should pass this gut check:

- Does the first frame read as a product, not a concept board?
- Does the shell still feel recognizably OpenAI?
- Does the stage feel like a block-set memory?
- Would someone want to send a screenshot before we explain anything?
